_Lambert's cosine law_ (or just Lambert's law in the notes) says that the observed radiant intensity or luminous intensity from an ideal diffusely reflecting surface is directly proportional to the cosine of the angle $\theta$ between the observer's line of sight and the surface normal.

![[Pasted image 20260319115336.png]]

Consider a light source with area $A$ and [[Flux|flux]] $\Phi$ that is illuminating a surface. [[Irradiance and Radiant Exitance|Irradiance]] at any point inside $A$ is 

Light shining directly down:
$$1=\frac{\Phi}{A}$$
Light shining at angle:
$$E=\frac{\Phi~cos\theta}{A}$$
