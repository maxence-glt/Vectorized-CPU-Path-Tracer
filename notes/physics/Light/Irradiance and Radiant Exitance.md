The average density of power over area $A$ is either 
- _irradiance_ (E), the area density of [[Flux|flux]] arriving at a surface
- _radiant exitance_ (M), the area density of [[Flux|flux]] leaving a surface. 
With the average density of power over the area as $E=\Phi/A$ 

These measurements have units of $W/m^2$.

![[Pasted image 20260319114247.png]]

Irradiance at a point on the outer sphere is less than the irradiance at a point on the inner sphere, since the surface area of the outer sphere is larger. In particular, if the point source is emitting the same amount of illumination in all directions, then for a sphere in this configuration that has radius $r$, $$E=\frac{\Phi}{4\pi r^2}$$
Which is why the amount of energy received from a light at a point falls off with the squared distance from the light.

To find power:
$$\Phi=\int_AE(p)dA$$
A good example is _[[Lambert's Cosine Law|Lambert's Law]]_
